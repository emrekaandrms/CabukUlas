# CabukUlas Brand and Design System Direction

## Brand Position
CabukUlas should feel like a calm, trusted access layer between people and large institutions. It is not a flashy consumer brand and not a bureaucratic utility either. The product should communicate:

- speed without urgency theater
- trust without corporate coldness
- warmth without childishness
- clarity without visual emptiness

## Emotional Tone
- Reassuring in stressful moments
- Warm and approachable in everyday browsing
- Quietly premium rather than loudly luxurious
- Competent, human, and editorially curated

## Visual Character
The recommended design language is "soft editorial utility":

- warm mineral backgrounds instead of stark white
- deep ink typography instead of pure black
- restrained amber accents for action and speed
- sage and rose accents for verification and caution
- surfaces that feel layered and tactile, not simply stacked cards

## Typography
- Display and screen titles should feel crafted and condensed enough to create identity.
- Body copy should remain highly legible for Turkish content and older users.
- Typography should do more of the branding work than decoration.
- Use weight, rhythm, and spacing to create hierarchy before using extra color.

## Surface Logic
- `canvas`: the full-screen atmospheric background
- `soft surface`: supportive contextual blocks
- `raised surface`: important interactive containers
- `accent surface`: recommended or high-confidence moments
- `contrast surface`: strongest decision points such as the recommended action

## Radius Logic
- Compact controls: 10-12
- Inputs and inline surfaces: 16
- Cards: 20-24
- Hero surfaces and branded blocks: 24-28
- Capsules only for chips, segmented controls, and status pills

## Motion Philosophy
- Motion should direct attention, not entertain.
- Entrance animations should be smooth and quick, never bouncy by default.
- Microinteractions should confirm user intent with subtle clarity.
- Splash motion should create a brand impression while masking minimal data preparation.

## Tactility
- Haptics should be used on best-action taps, favorite saves, cargo-submit, and confirmation moments.
- Press states should visibly compress or tint with intention, rather than simply reducing opacity everywhere.

## Trust Design Rules
- Every recommendation should have a reason nearby.
- Source quality must be visible, not implied.
- Freshness metadata should be readable without entering a deep settings view.
- Error and offline states should sound calm, practical, and non-technical.

## Anti-Generic Rules
- Avoid filling every screen with identical rounded white cards.
- Avoid turning every action into a filled primary button.
- Avoid decorative gradients unless they support a specific branded moment.
- Avoid overusing generic outline icons as the main source of personality.
- Avoid symmetrical stacked layouts that look like template-generated mobile UI.
